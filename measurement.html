What type of data we collect?

To expand the human presence in deep space, it is necessary to have human presence on the moon. The basic aspects that should be taken into account when designing lunar settlements are:

  • Location for shelter
  • Radiation
  • Temperatures
  • Solar irradiance
  • Excavation difficulties
  • Need of creating a pressurized habitat
  • Need of resources for survival and that allow sustainable expansion
For this reason, in this first mission we aim to search for lavatubes (good place for shelter) and water (useful for humans and rocket fuel).

Water finding

Water is an essential resource not only for survival but also for the development of fuel to be used in deep space exploration. For this reaseon the rover will be equipped with custom designed electrodes that inject current allowing to estimate soil resistivity up to a certain depth. This method required two or more rovers. The detection of anomalies in the soil resistivity may indicate the presence of water or ice.

Location for shelter

A excellent place for shelter are lava tubes, because it is a natural protection from radiation and meteorites and temperatures are very stable, ideal place for moon habits.
In this way, the rovers will use GPR and accelerometers. GPR is a technique based on the use of electromagnetic fields that allow the obtaining of images of the subsoil and accelerometers can be use for the deteccion of geological structures.
